How to export your data from Instagram

This converter expects the JSON “Download your information” package from Meta (Instagram), usually delivered as one or more .zip files. Meta’s menus and wording change over time; use the official links below if a label does not match exactly.

What you need: an export that includes your content in JSON form (not only HTML). Recent downloads use your_instagram_activity/media/posts_1.json (and posts_2.json, …) plus folders such as media/posts/. Older exports used a single media.json. Include those JSON files and the media/ tree when you zip the folder for this app.

Request a copy (Instagram app)

  1. Open Instagram and go to your profile.
  2. Open Settings (often the menu in the top corner).
  3. Find Your activity, Account, or Meta Accounts Center (wording depends on app version and region).
  4. Look for an option named like Download your information, Download a copy of your information, or Your information and permissions → Download your information.
  5. Choose Instagram (if asked which account), pick a date range and format. Select JSON if the export offers a format choice.
  6. Submit the request. Meta prepares the archive; this can take hours to days. You may get an email when it is ready.
  7. Download the ZIP file(s) to your computer. If the export is split into multiple parts, download all parts—you can select every part ZIP on the converter home page.

Request a copy (web / Accounts Center)

You can also start or manage a download from Meta’s account tools in a browser:

  • Meta Accounts Center — Download your information
  • Instagram Privacy Center — Download your information (paths may redirect to Accounts Center)
