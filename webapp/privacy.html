Privacy policy

Last updated: May 4, 2026

This page describes how the Instagram Dump Converter static web app (the pages and scripts served from this site) handles information when you use it.

Summary

The converter runs entirely in your web browser. When you select Instagram export ZIP files, they are read and processed locally. We do not upload your export, posts, media, or ZIP files to our servers, because this app is not designed to send that data anywhere. There is no account system on this site for the converter itself.

What we do not collect for the converter

For the core “pick ZIP → generate HTML / download ZIP” flow, we do not intentionally collect, store, or process your Instagram content on a server we operate for that purpose. Your files stay on your device unless you choose to save or share them elsewhere.

What may still leave your device

  • Third-party scripts (CDN). This app loads JavaScript libraries from a public CDN (for example esm.sh) to unzip and build files in the browser. Those providers may receive standard technical data (such as IP address and request metadata) as part of normal HTTPS requests, according to their own policies.
  • Fonts. If you load pages that pull fonts from Google Fonts, Google may receive typical web request data associated with font delivery.
  • Hosting and logs. Whoever hosts this static site (you, a provider, etc.) may keep web server or analytics logs (URLs visited, timestamps, IP addresses) like any normal website. That is outside the converter’s own code.

Cookies

The converter does not rely on cookies for its main behavior. Your host or analytics tools, if any, may set cookies according to their configuration.

Children

This tool is not directed at children. If you are a parent or guardian and believe a child has used the service in a way that concerns you, contact the site operator.

Changes

We may update this policy when the app or hosting changes. The “Last updated” date at the top will be revised when we do.

Contact

For privacy questions about this deployment, contact the person or organization that published this site (repository maintainer or host). This project is open source; see the project README for how to reach maintainers.
